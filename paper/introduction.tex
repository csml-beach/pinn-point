\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) have emerged as a powerful paradigm for solving partial differential equations (PDEs) by embedding physical constraints directly into the neural network's loss function. However, the accuracy and convergence of PINNs are heavily dependent on the distribution of collocation points where the PDE residuals are evaluated. This dependency is particularly pronounced in problems involving complex geometries, sharp gradients, or multi-scale phenomena, where uniform or random sampling often fails to capture localized features, leading to slow training or poor generalization.

A growing body of literature has explored adaptive sampling strategies to address these challenges. These methods typically aim to concentrate collocation points in regions with high PDE residuals, effectively "focusing" the network's attention on the most difficult parts of the domain. While residual-based methods like the Residual-based Adaptive Distribution (RAD) \cite{wu2023comprehensive} show promise, they often suffer from numerical instabilities—such as NaN gradients or probability collapse—and can lack the necessary spatial diversity to prevent the model from over-fitting to narrow regions of the domain.

In this work, we propose a new family of adaptive sampling strategies specifically designed for robustness and efficiency in complex 2D geometries. Our approach, rooted in \textbf{power-tempered residual guidance} and \textbf{entropy-balanced sampling}, utilizes a mesh-based scaffold to integrate residual information across the domain. By tempering the residual distribution and maintaining an explicit focus on spatial diversity, our methods provide a stable and high-fidelity alternative to existing adaptive strategies.

We evaluate our proposed methods using the \textbf{\pinnpoint} framework, a comprehensive benchmarking suite that ensures a "fair comparison" between sampling strategies. By sharing fixed FEM-derived supervised data, reference solutions, and matched iteration budgets, we isolate the impact of the collocation policy itself. Our results across a suite of challenging PDEs—including Navier-Stokes, Advection-Diffusion, and Allen-Cahn equations—demonstrate that our adaptive strategies significantly improve convergence rates and final error accuracy compared to established baselines.

The main contributions of this work are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item A novel adaptive sampling methodology based on power-tempered and entropy-balanced residual distributions.
    \item A robust mesh-guided implementation that ensures stability and spatial diversity even in complex, perforated domains.
    \item A comprehensive benchmarking study across multiple PDEs (Poisson, Navier-Stokes, Advection-Diffusion, and Allen-Cahn) highlighting the trade-offs between various sampling policies.
    \item Evidence-based results showing significant improvements in error-to-cost efficiency on `m3-large-cpu` and GPU targets.
\end{itemize}
