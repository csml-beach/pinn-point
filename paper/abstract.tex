Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) offer a promising approach for solving partial differential equations (PDEs), yet their performance is highly sensitive to the distribution of collocation points, especially in complex 2D geometries. We propose a new family of adaptive sampling strategies based on power-tempered and entropy-balanced residual guidance. By utilizing a mesh-based scaffold to integrate residual information, our methods maintain spatial diversity while concentrating collocation points in regions of high error. We evaluate these strategies using the \pinnpoint benchmarking framework across multiple challenging PDEs, including Navier-Stokes and Advection-Diffusion equations. Our results show that these adaptive methods provide more robust convergence and higher final accuracy than established baselines, while adding minimal computational overhead.
